Button Smashing

An attempt to imagine a massive multiplayer offline game

Empowering people to meet new people by competing with their best friend.



Project Info Skills
4 Weeks Project Research
Object & Space class Prototyping
Interaction Design at CCA Interaction Design






Introduction

It is very easy for us to cling to technology devices when in awkward social situations. Having a reason to initiate a conversation can often make the process simple.

Focus

Understand effective ways to encourage simple social interactions. Prototype a proof of concept with accessible materials and tools such as laser cutting and photon.

Approach

Content synthesis from interviews with two Industrial Design third-years. Because they have very specific habits of avoiding social interactions besides interactions with each other, we wanted to increase the possibility of them interacting with classmates in school.


Narrowing the idea

We decided to make a simple game that is Internet-connected. Each player can invite anyone to play a 30-second button-smashing game. If the winner is the owner of the game, a text message will be sent to his or her partner, informing them of the win and encouraging the partner to try to beat him or her.


Prototyping

The process involved 3d modeling the appearance of the game, building and testing the electronics, and having the device to text the other partner's phone via IFTTT.


Assembling

After experiementing with several versions enclosures, and refining the code for the game, everything was then assembled.
