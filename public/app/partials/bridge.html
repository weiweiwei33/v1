Button Up

An attempt to imagine a massive multiplayer offline game

Introduction

It is very easy to turn toward the devices we carry at many different kinds of social environments. Having an alibi to initiate a conversation can often make the process simple.

Focus

Understand effective ways to encourage simple social interactions. Prototype a proof of concept with accessible materials and tools such as laser cutting and photon.

Approach

see documentation

First things first

Content synthesis from interviews with two Industrial Design third-years. Because they have very specific habits of avoiding social interactions besides interactions with each other, we wanted to increase the possibility of them interacting with classmates in school.

Narrowing the idea

We decided to make a simple game that is Internet-connected. Each player can invite anyone to play a 30-second button-smashing game and, if the winner is the owner of the game, a text message will be sent to his or her partner, informing them of the win and encouraging the partner to not even try to win.
