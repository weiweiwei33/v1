Energy Systems

Integrating car batteries into the energy grid

As a sponsored project, we looked at how Ford might elegantly mitigate the impact of its energy dependent products and increasing private clean energy solutions in the city of San Francisco.

Introduction

In day to day life, we do not communicate information about the energy systems we are apart of. No matter where we live, we are all likely to have a poor understanding of how we create pollution footprint; there isn’t a nutrition label for energy on the products we purchase, there isn't a stock ticker for the price of electricity. While all of the products we use are energy dependent, we are not leveraging technology to create an efficient energy network. In a team of three, we reimagined the way we consume, store, and distribute energy.



Approach

Synthesizing secondary research materials mainly from Saul Griffith, Bret Victor, and David Mackey, we understood that:

  • As individuals, we must budget our lifestyle to only consume 12,000 watt per year.

  • In order to fully traisition into a renewable energy era, we have to optimize the infrastructure for energy storage.

    Delivery

    Through leveraging the portability of smarter car batteries, the final proposal of this project was to relieve energy grid strain. By using the sharing economy to transport and store energy, it also intend to leverage the embedded energy of cars to a greater degree.
